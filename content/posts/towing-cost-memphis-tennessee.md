> **Quick Answer:** Memphis towing runs $75-120 for hookup plus $3-5 per mile. After-hours adds $25-50. Highway calls cost more due to safety risks. AAA members save 20-30%. Wait times average 45-90 minutes depending on location and time of day.

## What Towing Costs in Memphis

Base hookup fees in Memphis range from $75-120 for standard vehicles during business hours. The per-mile rate typically runs $3-5, with most local tows staying under $150 total.

After-hours service (nights, weekends, holidays) adds a $25-50 surcharge. Highway towing on I-40, I-240, or I-269 costs $20-40 more than surface street calls due to safety equipment requirements and traffic hazards.

Motorcycle towing runs $60-90 for hookup. Heavy-duty trucks and RVs start at $150-200 with higher per-mile rates of $6-8. Flatbed service adds $15-25 to any call but prevents further damage to disabled vehicles.

Storage fees at Memphis tow yards run $20-35 per day after the first 24 hours. Impound fees from Memphis Police Department add $100-150 on top of towing costs.

## What To Do When You Break Down in Memphis

1. **Get to safety immediately.** If on I-40 or I-240, exit at the next ramp if possible. These interstates see heavy truck traffic and frequent accidents. Use hazards and stay in your vehicle if stuck in lanes.

2. **Call 911 first if on highways.** Tennessee Highway Patrol responds to interstate breakdowns and can arrange safe towing. They'll position behind your vehicle for protection while you wait.

3. **Contact AAA or your insurance roadside coverage.** AAA has strong Memphis coverage through Roadway Towing and several other providers. Average wait time is 60 minutes for members, 90+ for non-members.

4. **For immediate service, call local operators.** Panda Towing, A&A Towing, and Pro Towing Service cover Memphis 24/7. Provide exact location using mile markers on interstates or nearest cross streets in the city.

5. **Expect longer waits during rush hours (7-9 AM, 4-6 PM) and Grizzlies game days.** Traffic congestion around downtown and near FedExForum significantly delays tow trucks.

## Local Tips for Memphis

• Avoid breaking down on the Hernando DeSoto Bridge (I-40 over Mississippi River). Limited shoulder space makes this extremely dangerous. If your car starts having issues approaching the bridge, take the downtown Memphis exit instead.

• Summer heat reaches 95°F+ regularly. Keep water in your car and crack windows while waiting. Memphis humidity makes it feel even hotter inside vehicles.

• Beezely Street and parts of Lamar Avenue see higher crime rates. Lock doors, keep windows cracked only slightly, and don't display valuables while waiting for tows in these areas.

• Memphis impounds vehicles quickly from certain zones. Poplar Avenue, Union Avenue, and areas near the Medical District have strict parking enforcement and rapid towing for violations.

• Flash flooding hits Memphis fast during spring storms. Avoid low-lying areas like parts of Shelby Drive and areas near Wolf River tributaries when weather threatens.

## What Affects the Final Price

• **Vehicle type matters significantly.** Low-clearance sports cars require flatbeds, adding $15-25. Large SUVs and trucks cost more due to weight and positioning challenges.

• **Distance drives the total cost.** Memphis spans a large area. Towing from Cordova to Millington can add $40-60 in mileage charges over a short downtown tow.

• **Time of service impacts pricing.** Late night calls (11 PM - 6 AM) cost the most. Holiday surcharges can reach $75 extra on major holidays.

• **Membership discounts provide real savings.** AAA, AARP, and some auto insurance policies cut costs by 20-30%. Credit union roadside programs often work with local Memphis operators.

• **Insurance coverage varies widely.** Some policies cover full towing costs, others cap at $50-100. Check your coverage before you need it.

## Stay Safe

• Stay inside your vehicle with doors locked, especially at night or in unfamiliar areas. Memphis has pockets of higher crime that make waiting outside risky.

• Keep your phone charged and share your location with family or friends. Cell coverage is generally good throughout Memphis, but battery life matters during long waits.

• Position reflective triangles or flares 100 feet behind your vehicle if you have them. Memphis drivers often speed and may not see disabled vehicles until too late.

• If approached by strangers offering help, politely decline and stay in your locked car. Wait for uniformed tow truck operators or police officers only.
