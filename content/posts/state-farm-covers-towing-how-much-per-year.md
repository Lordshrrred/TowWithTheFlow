> **Quick Answer:** State Farm's roadside assistance typically covers $100-200 per towing incident with no annual dollar limit, but limits the number of service calls per year. Standard coverage includes 4-6 service calls annually, while enhanced plans may offer unlimited services.

## What Your State Farm Towing Coverage Includes

State Farm offers roadside assistance through two main options:

1. **Basic Roadside Coverage**: Usually 4 service calls per year, $100-150 per tow
2. **Enhanced/Premium Coverage**: 6+ service calls per year or unlimited, $150-200+ per tow

The annual limit isn't a dollar amount. It's based on service incidents.

## What To Do When You Need Towing

1. Call State Farm's roadside number: 1-877-SF-CLAIM (1-877-732-5246)
2. Provide your policy number and location
3. Specify you need towing service
4. Ask for confirmation of your remaining service calls for the year
5. Get the tow truck company's name and estimated arrival time
6. Keep all receipts if you pay upfront

## Coverage Details That Matter

**Distance limits**: Most policies cover towing to the nearest qualified repair facility, typically 15-25 miles included.

**Excess distance**: You pay per-mile charges beyond the included distance, usually $3-5 per mile.

**Service call resets**: Your annual service calls reset on your policy anniversary date, not January 1st.

## What It Might Cost

- **Monthly premium**: $15-35 added to your auto policy
- **Out-of-pocket towing**: $75-200+ without coverage
- **Excess mileage**: $3-5 per mile beyond included distance

## When Coverage Doesn't Apply

State Farm roadside assistance excludes:
- Towing due to accidents (covered under collision/comprehensive)
- Commercial vehicles over certain weight limits
- Off-road recovery in some policies
- Towing to your preferred shop if farther than nearest facility
