> **Quick Answer:** Sunday night tow truck service for a flat tire costs $125-250 depending on distance and location. If you just need a tire change, roadside assistance runs $75-150. Always call multiple companies and ask about flat tire service before requesting a full tow.

## What To Do

1. **Check if you really need towing.** Many tow companies offer tire changing services for less money than a full tow.

2. **Call your insurance company first.** Many auto policies include roadside assistance that covers Sunday nights at no extra charge.

3. **Try AAA or roadside apps.** If you have membership, use it. Non-members can often pay on the spot for $100-150.

4. **Get quotes from multiple tow companies.** Sunday night rates vary wildly between companies. Call at least three.

5. **Ask specifically about tire change service.** Say "I have a flat tire and need roadside help" instead of "I need a tow."

6. **Confirm the total cost upfront.** Get mileage rates, base fees, and Sunday surcharges in writing via text.

7. **Have your spare tire accessible.** If it's locked under the truck bed or buried under cargo, move it before the truck arrives.

## What It Might Cost

**Tire Change Service:**
- Base rate: $60-100
- Sunday/holiday surcharge: $15-50
- Total: $75-150

**Full Tow Service:**
- Base hook-up: $75-125
- Per-mile rate: $3-7
- Sunday surcharge: $25-75
- 5-mile tow total: $125-250

**Insurance/AAA:**
- Often covered completely
- May have small deductible ($25-50)

## Stay Safe

- Pull completely off the roadway before calling
- Turn on hazard lights and use flares if available
- Stay inside your vehicle if on a busy road
- Have your license, registration, and payment ready
- Verify the tow truck company name matches who you called
