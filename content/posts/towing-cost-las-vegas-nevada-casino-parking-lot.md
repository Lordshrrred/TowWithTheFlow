> **Quick Answer:** Casino parking lot towing in Las Vegas typically costs $150-400 for the initial tow, plus $20-50 daily storage fees. Major Strip casinos contract with specific tow companies that set their own rates. You'll need ID, proof of ownership, and cash or card to retrieve your vehicle.

## What To Do

1. **Call the casino security desk immediately** - They'll confirm which tow company has your car and provide the lot address.

2. **Gather required documents** - Driver's license, vehicle registration, and insurance card. Some lots accept photos of documents on your phone.

3. **Get the exact address** - Las Vegas has multiple tow yards. Going to the wrong one wastes time and money on rideshares.

4. **Bring payment method** - Most accept credit cards now, but some require cash. Call ahead to confirm.

5. **Arrive during business hours** - Most tow yards close by 6 PM weekdays and have limited weekend hours. After-hours retrieval adds $50-100.

6. **Inspect your vehicle before leaving** - Document any damage immediately. Take photos if needed.

## What It Might Cost

**Initial towing fee:** $150-250
**Administrative fee:** $25-75
**Daily storage:** $20-50 per day (starts immediately)
**After-hours retrieval:** $50-100 extra

Popular Strip casinos like Bellagio, MGM, and Caesars typically charge on the higher end. Downtown and off-Strip locations may cost less.

Storage fees accumulate daily, including weekends and holidays. A car towed Friday evening costs storage through Monday pickup.

## Stay Safe

• Never argue with tow truck drivers - They're contracted workers following casino orders
• Don't leave valuables visible in your car - Tow yards aren't responsible for theft
• Take photos of your car's location before walking away - Helps prove you parked legally if disputing
• Keep parking receipts - Some casinos tow cars that look abandoned even in legal spots
• Know casino parking rules - Free parking often has time limits or requires validation
