> **Quick Answer:** Pull over immediately and turn off the engine. Do not open the hood if you see flames or heavy smoke. Call 911 for fire, or a tow truck for mechanical issues. In Miami's heat, engine overheating is common and can cause serious damage if you keep driving.

## What To Do

1. **Pull over safely** to the right shoulder or parking lot. Turn on hazard lights.

2. **Turn off the engine immediately** and remove the key from ignition.

3. **Exit the vehicle** and move away from the car. Stand upwind from any smoke.

4. **Assess the situation:**
   - White/gray steam: likely coolant leak or overheating
   - Black/dark smoke: oil burning or serious engine damage
   - Any flames visible: call 911 immediately

5. **Do not open the hood** if smoke is heavy or you suspect fire. Hot coolant can spray and cause severe burns.

6. **Call for help:**
   - Fire/flames: 911 first
   - Mechanical issues: tow truck or roadside assistance

7. **If safe to check:** Wait 15-20 minutes for cooling, then carefully open hood with a cloth or tool. Look for obvious leaks, broken hoses, or damaged components.

## Stay Safe

• Never ignore smoke from the hood
• Keep fire extinguisher in your car
• Miami heat makes overheating worse, check coolant levels regularly
• Do not add cold water to a hot engine
• Stay clear of the vehicle if smoke continues after shutdown
• Have your phone charged for emergency calls
