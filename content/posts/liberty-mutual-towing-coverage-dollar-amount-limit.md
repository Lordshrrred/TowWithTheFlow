> **Quick Answer:** Liberty Mutual's standard towing coverage typically covers $100-150 per incident. Enhanced roadside assistance plans can provide $200-500 coverage. Your exact limit is listed on your policy declarations page under "Emergency Road Service" or "Roadside Assistance."

## What To Do

1. **Check your declarations page immediately** - Look for "Emergency Road Service," "Roadside Assistance," or "Towing Coverage" sections
2. **Call Liberty Mutual directly** at 1-800-290-8711 to verify your current coverage limits
3. **Ask the tow truck driver for an estimate** before they hook up your vehicle
4. **Get a written receipt** showing the total towing cost and distance
5. **Submit your claim online** through Liberty Mutual's app or website with your receipt
6. **Pay any amount over your limit** out of pocket at the time of service

## Coverage Details by Policy Type

**Basic Auto Policy:**
- Usually $100-150 per towing incident
- Covers towing to nearest qualified repair facility
- May include one free tow per policy year

**Enhanced Roadside Assistance:**
- Typically $200-500 per incident
- Higher annual limits (often 3-4 incidents)
- May cover towing to facility of your choice

**Platinum Coverage:**
- Up to $500+ per incident
- Unlimited distance to preferred repair shop
- Additional perks like rental car coordination

## What It Might Cost

Standard towing runs $75-125 for local distances. Long-distance tows can hit $200-400+. If your Liberty Mutual limit is $150 and your tow costs $275, you pay $125 out of pocket.

## Important Notes

- Distance matters: Coverage may specify "nearest qualified facility"
- Some policies have annual limits, not just per-incident limits
- Luxury or specialty vehicles may exceed standard coverage quickly
- Weekend and holiday towing often costs more
