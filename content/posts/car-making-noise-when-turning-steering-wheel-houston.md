> **Quick Answer:** Steering wheel noise when turning usually means low power steering fluid, a worn power steering belt, failing CV joints, or bad steering components. Stop driving immediately if the steering feels hard or unresponsive. Check power steering fluid first, then get professional diagnosis.

## What To Do

1. **Pull over safely** if steering becomes difficult or makes loud grinding sounds
2. **Check power steering fluid** under the hood - look for the reservoir with a steering wheel symbol
3. **Listen to the noise type**:
   - Squealing = likely belt or pump issue
   - Clicking/popping = probably CV joints
   - Groaning/whining = usually low fluid or pump failure
4. **Test at idle** - turn wheel left and right while parked to isolate the sound
5. **Check for leaks** under the car where you normally park
6. **Get to a shop immediately** if fluid is low, steering is hard, or noise is getting worse

## What It Might Cost

- Power steering fluid top-off: $15-30
- Power steering belt replacement: $80-150
- Power steering pump: $300-600
- CV joint replacement: $400-800 per side
- Tie rod ends: $200-400

Houston's heat makes power steering problems worse. Don't wait.

## Stay Safe

- Never ignore hard steering or loud grinding
- Keep power steering fluid in your car for emergencies
- Houston traffic demands responsive steering
