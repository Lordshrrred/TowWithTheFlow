> **Quick Answer:** Allstate roadside assistance typically covers towing up to 15 miles from your breakdown location to the nearest qualified repair facility. Some premium plans may offer 100+ mile coverage, but standard coverage is limited. Distance varies by your specific policy.

## What To Do

1. **Check your policy documents** - Look for your roadside assistance certificate or log into your Allstate account online to see your exact towing distance limit.

2. **Call Allstate roadside assistance** at 1-800-ALLSTATE (1-800-255-7828) when you need a tow. They'll verify your coverage limits.

3. **Ask about your destination options** - They'll tow to the nearest qualified repair shop within your covered distance, not necessarily where you want to go.

4. **Request distance calculation** - Ask the dispatcher how far your preferred shop is from your location before agreeing to the tow.

5. **Pay the difference if needed** - If you want to go beyond your covered distance, you'll pay the additional mileage cost directly to the tow truck driver.

6. **Consider upgrading coverage** - If 15 miles isn't enough, ask about premium roadside plans that offer 100+ mile coverage.

## What It Might Cost

Standard Allstate roadside assistance covers the first 15 miles at no charge. Beyond that distance, expect to pay $3-7 per additional mile depending on your location and the towing company rates.

Premium roadside plans with extended towing coverage typically add $20-40 annually to your insurance premium.

## Stay Safe

- Don't assume unlimited towing distance
- Confirm coverage limits before the tow truck arrives
- Ask for the total estimated cost if going beyond covered distance
- Keep cash or card ready for overage charges
- Get written confirmation of final destination before the tow begins
