> **Quick Answer:** Some insurance policies have time restrictions on towing coverage, typically excluding service between midnight and 6 AM. Check your policy details immediately. If you're stranded after midnight, you'll likely pay out of pocket, then seek reimbursement if your policy allows it.

## What To Do

1. **Call your insurance company first** - Even with time restrictions, they may have emergency exceptions or can authorize payment for immediate safety concerns.

2. **Document everything** - Take photos of your vehicle, location, and time stamps. Save all receipts and get detailed invoices from the towing company.

3. **Choose a reputable tow service** - Ask for upfront pricing. Explain your insurance situation to avoid surprise charges.

4. **Request itemized billing** - Get separate charges for hookup, mileage, and any storage fees clearly listed.

5. **File for reimbursement** - Submit your claim with all documentation as soon as possible, even if the tow happened during excluded hours.

6. **Contact your insurance again** - Some companies will reimburse after-hours towing costs up to your policy limits, even if they don't provide direct service.

## Why This Happens

Insurance companies limit midnight coverage because:
- Higher costs for after-hours service calls
- Increased risk of fraudulent claims
- Limited network of available tow operators
- Safety concerns for drivers in remote areas

## What It Might Cost

Expect to pay $100-300 for basic towing after midnight. Emergency surcharges can add 25-50% to standard rates. Long-distance tows or difficult extractions cost significantly more.

## Stay Safe

• Never attempt roadside repairs in the dark on busy roads
• Keep your doors locked while waiting for help
• Stay in your vehicle if it's safer than standing outside
• Have emergency cash or cards ready for immediate payment
• Consider upgrading to 24-hour coverage for future incidents
