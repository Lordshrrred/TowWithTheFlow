> **Quick Answer:** Houston 24-hour towing typically costs $50-100 extra for after-hours service. Base towing runs $75-125, so expect $125-225 total for nights, weekends, and holidays. Distance and vehicle type affect the final price.

## What To Do

1. **Call multiple tow companies** to compare after-hours rates. Prices vary significantly between operators.

2. **Ask for the total cost upfront** including base rate, after-hours fee, and mileage charges. Don't accept vague estimates.

3. **Check if your insurance covers towing** through roadside assistance. Many policies include 24-hour service at no extra cost.

4. **Verify the company is licensed** with the Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation before agreeing to service.

5. **Get the driver's name and truck number** when they arrive. Legitimate operators always provide this information.

6. **Pay only after your vehicle is delivered** to the destination. Never pay upfront for towing services.

## What It Might Cost

**Standard Houston towing rates:**
- Daytime towing: $75-125 base rate
- After-hours surcharge: $50-100 extra
- Mileage: $3-5 per mile beyond first 5-10 miles
- Heavy vehicles (trucks, SUVs): Additional $25-50

**Total 24-hour towing cost: $125-225**

Weekend and holiday rates often match after-hours pricing. Some companies charge flat rates for short distances, others use mileage from pickup.

**Insurance typically covers:**
- AAA: Up to 4 service calls per year
- Auto insurance roadside: Usually 24-hour coverage included
- Credit card benefits: Check your card's emergency services

## Stay Safe

• Stay in your vehicle if on a busy road until help arrives
• Turn on hazard lights and use flares or reflective triangles
• Never accept rides from unlicensed tow operators
• Keep your doors locked until the legitimate tow truck arrives
• Take photos of your vehicle before it's loaded for transport
• Ask for the destination address and estimated arrival time
