> **Quick Answer:** AAA members pay $0 for covered tows (up to mileage limits), while non-AAA towing runs $75-150 in Chicago and $65-135 in Phoenix. AAA Basic covers 4 tows per year up to 3 miles. Non-AAA costs vary widely by distance and company.

## What To Do

1. **Check your AAA membership status** - Log into the AAA app or call member services to verify coverage and available tows remaining

2. **For AAA members** - Call AAA directly at 1-800-AAA-HELP, provide your membership number and exact location

3. **For non-AAA emergencies** - Get quotes from 2-3 local tow companies before choosing, as prices vary significantly

4. **Ask about distance charges** - Both cities charge extra beyond base service areas (typically 3-5 miles)

5. **Verify total cost upfront** - Request written estimate including all fees before authorizing service

## What It Might Cost

**AAA Members:**
- Basic: 4 free tows up to 3 miles per year
- Plus: 4 free tows up to 100 miles per year  
- Premier: 1 free 200-mile tow, plus 3 free 100-mile tows per year

**Non-AAA Chicago:**
- Base tow (up to 5 miles): $85-150
- Each additional mile: $3-7
- After-hours surcharge: $15-35
- Storage fees: $25-45 per day

**Non-AAA Phoenix:**
- Base tow (up to 5 miles): $65-135  
- Each additional mile: $2-6
- Weekend/holiday surcharge: $20-40
- Storage fees: $20-40 per day

**Hidden costs to watch for:**
- Hookup fees ($25-50)
- Fuel surcharges ($5-15)
- Credit card processing fees (3-5%)

## Stay Safe

• Never leave your vehicle unattended with the tow truck driver
• Take photos of your car before it's loaded
• Get the driver's name, company info, and destination address in writing  
• Keep all receipts for insurance claims
• Ask where your vehicle will be stored and what the access hours are
• Verify the tow truck company is licensed and insured before service begins
