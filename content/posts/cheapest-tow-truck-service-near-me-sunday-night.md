> **Quick Answer:** Sunday night towing costs 20-40% more than weekday rates. Call multiple 24-hour services, check your insurance roadside coverage first, and expect $75-150 for local tows. AAA and insurance company partnerships often beat independent operators on weekends.

## What To Do

1. **Check your insurance coverage first.** Many auto policies include roadside assistance that costs less than cash rates, especially on Sundays.

2. **Call AAA if you're a member.** Even basic membership ($60/year) beats most Sunday night towing fees for a single call.

3. **Use Google Maps or Yelp to search "24 hour tow truck near me."** Read recent reviews focusing on Sunday/weekend experiences.

4. **Call 3-5 companies for quotes.** Rates vary dramatically. Ask for the total cost including mileage, not just the hook-up fee.

5. **Ask dispatchers about cash discounts.** Some operators knock off $10-20 for immediate payment.

6. **Check if local police can recommend services.** Non-emergency lines often have relationships with fair-priced operators.

7. **Consider ride-share home and Monday pickup.** If your car is safely parked, waiting until Monday can save $30-60.

## What It Might Cost

Sunday night premium adds 20-40% to base rates:

- **Local tow (under 10 miles):** $75-150
- **Longer hauls:** $3-7 per mile after base fee
- **Flatbed service:** Add $20-40
- **Insurance/AAA members:** Often $0-50

Cash typically beats credit cards by $10-20.

## Stay Safe

- Never accept rides from unlicensed operators
- Verify the company name matches what you called
- Get written estimates before work begins
- Stay with your vehicle until help arrives
- Keep your phone charged and share your location
- If stranded on highways, get away from traffic and stay visible
