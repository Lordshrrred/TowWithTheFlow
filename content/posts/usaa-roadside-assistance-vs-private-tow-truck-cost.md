> **Quick Answer:** USAA roadside assistance costs $15-25 per year and covers most towing up to 15-25 miles. Private tow trucks charge $75-150 for hookup plus $2-7 per mile. USAA wins for members who need towing more than once every few years.

## What To Do

1. **Check your USAA policy first.** Log into your account or call to see if you already have roadside coverage. Many comprehensive policies include it automatically.

2. **Know USAA's limits.** Standard coverage includes 15 miles of towing, jump starts, lockouts, and flat tire changes. Enhanced coverage extends to 25 miles and adds fuel delivery.

3. **Calculate private tow costs.** Local tow trucks typically charge $75-125 hookup fee plus $2-4 per mile in rural areas, $4-7 per mile in cities. A 10-mile tow runs $95-195.

4. **Factor in your driving habits.** Drive an older car? Take long trips? Park in sketchy areas? You'll likely use roadside assistance multiple times over several years.

5. **Consider wait times.** USAA contracts with local providers but you might wait 45-90 minutes. Private companies you call directly often arrive faster, especially in busy areas.

6. **Read the fine print.** USAA covers most passenger vehicles but excludes motorcycles, RVs over certain weights, and vehicles used for business. Private tow trucks handle anything.

## What It Might Cost

**USAA Annual Premium:**
- Basic roadside: $15-20 per year
- Enhanced coverage: $20-30 per year

**Private Tow Truck (typical 10-mile tow):**
- Small cities: $95-135
- Major metro areas: $125-195
- Rural areas: $75-115

**Break-even point:** If you need towing twice in five years, USAA saves money. One major breakdown and you're ahead.

## Stay Safe

• Keep your USAA membership card and policy number in your wallet
• Save USAA's 24/7 roadside number: 800-531-USAA
• Have backup cash for situations USAA won't cover
• Know that private tow trucks accept credit cards but USAA billing goes through your policy
• In dangerous locations, call police first, then your tow service
