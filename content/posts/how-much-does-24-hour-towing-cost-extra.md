> **Quick Answer:** 24 hour towing services typically charge $25-75 extra beyond standard daytime rates. Night surcharges (6 PM-6 AM) average $25-50, while weekend and holiday fees can add $50-75. Total emergency towing costs range from $100-300 depending on distance and vehicle type.

## What To Do

1. **Call multiple tow companies** to compare 24 hour rates before choosing
2. **Ask for the total cost breakdown** including base rate, mileage, and after-hours fees
3. **Check if your insurance covers emergency towing** to reduce out-of-pocket costs
4. **Verify the hookup location** since some companies charge extra to travel outside city limits
5. **Get a written estimate** if possible, especially for long-distance tows
6. **Confirm payment methods** as some 24 hour services only accept cash or cards

## What It Might Cost

**Standard after-hours surcharges:**
- Night service (6 PM-6 AM): $25-50 extra
- Weekend rates: $30-60 extra
- Holiday towing: $50-75 extra
- Emergency callout fee: $15-35

**Total typical costs:**
- Local tow (under 10 miles): $100-200
- Medium distance (10-50 miles): $150-250
- Long distance (50+ miles): $200-350+

**Factors that increase costs:**
- Heavy vehicles (trucks, RVs): 25-50% more
- Difficult recovery situations: $50-150 extra
- Flatbed vs. dolly towing: $25-40 difference

## Stay Safe

• Stay in your vehicle if on a busy highway until the tow truck arrives
• Turn on hazard lights and use road flares or reflectors if you have them
• Move to the shoulder as far right as possible
• Exit from the side away from traffic when the tow truck arrives
• Keep your phone charged for communication with the towing company
• Have your insurance and registration ready for the driver
