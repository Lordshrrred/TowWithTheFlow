> **Quick Answer:** AAA members in Chicago pay $0-200 for night towing depending on membership level and distance. Non-AAA customers face $150-400+ with surge pricing, limited availability, and cash-only operators common after midnight.

## What To Do

1. **Check your AAA status first** - dig through your wallet, check the app, or call 800-222-4357 to verify active membership

2. **Call AAA immediately if you're a member** - average wait times in Chicago are 45-90 minutes at night, but you're locked into their contracted rates

3. **For non-AAA situations, get quotes from multiple companies**:
   - Call at least 3 different tow services
   - Ask for total price upfront including night surcharges
   - Confirm they accept credit cards (many night operators are cash-only)

4. **Avoid roadside "helpers"** - never accept services from someone who just shows up, especially on I-90, I-94, or the Dan Ryan

5. **Document everything** - take photos of your car's position and any damage before the tow truck arrives

## What It Might Cost

**AAA Members:**
- Basic: First 3 miles free, then $3-4 per mile
- Plus: First 100 miles free
- Premier: First 200 miles free
- Service call fee: $0 with valid membership

**Non-AAA Chicago Night Rates:**
- Base tow: $125-200
- Night surcharge (6 PM - 6 AM): $25-75 additional  
- Mileage: $3-6 per mile after first 5-10 miles
- Storage fees: $25-45 per day if going to their lot
- Highway/expressway pickups: $50-100 additional fee

Distance matters. Towing from downtown to suburbs can easily hit $300-400 without AAA.

## Stay Safe

- Keep your doors locked until the legitimate tow truck arrives
- Stay in your vehicle if it's safely off the roadway
- Turn on hazard lights and use road flares if you have them
- Position yourself away from traffic if you must exit the vehicle
- Never sign blank forms or pay before seeing the total itemized bill
- Request the driver's license and company certification before allowing the tow
