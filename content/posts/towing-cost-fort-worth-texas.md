> **Quick Answer:** Fort Worth towing runs $85-120 for the base hookup, plus $3-5 per mile. After-hours service (nights, weekends, holidays) adds $25-50. Highway calls cost more than local streets. Total for a typical 10-mile tow: $115-170 during business hours, $140-220 after hours.

## What Towing Costs in Fort Worth

Base hookup fees in Fort Worth range from $85-120 for standard passenger vehicles. The per-mile rate runs $3-5, depending on the company and your location within the metroplex.

After-hours surcharges add $25-50 to your bill. This applies evenings after 6 PM, weekends, and holidays. Highway calls on I-35W, I-30, or Loop 820 typically cost $10-20 more than local street pickups due to safety equipment requirements and traffic hazards.

Motorcycle towing starts around $75-95 base, while heavy-duty service for trucks or RVs jumps to $150-200 base with higher per-mile rates of $5-8.

Cash payments sometimes get you a 5-10% discount, while credit card processing may add a small fee.

## What To Do When You Break Down in Fort Worth

1. **Get to safety immediately.** If on I-35W or I-30, try to reach an exit or pull far right onto the shoulder. Fort Worth traffic moves fast, especially during rush hours (7-9 AM, 4-7 PM).

2. **Call 911 first if you're on a major highway.** Texas Department of Public Safety monitors I-35W, I-30, and other state highways. They'll dispatch assistance and can help with traffic control.

3. **Contact your insurance or AAA.** Most auto insurance includes roadside coverage. AAA members get priority service with typical wait times of 45-60 minutes in Fort Worth during normal hours.

4. **For immediate service, call local Fort Worth towing companies.** Response times average 30-45 minutes for local streets, 45-75 minutes for highway calls during peak hours.

5. **Know your exact location.** Use mile markers on highways or cross streets in the city. Popular breakdown spots include the I-35W/I-30 mixmaster downtown and the Loop 820 corridor.

## Local Tips for Fort Worth

• **Avoid the mixmaster during rush hour.** The I-35W/I-30 interchange downtown backs up severely. If possible, limp to surface streets before calling for help.

• **Summer heat kills batteries and overheats engines.** July and August see the highest breakdown rates. Carry extra water and stay hydrated while waiting.

• **Construction zones change frequently.** I-35W and US 287 often have active work zones with reduced speeds and narrow lanes, making towing more expensive and dangerous.

• **Impound rules are strict.** Fort Worth police will tow abandoned vehicles within 48 hours on city streets, 24 hours on highways. Don't leave your car overnight without notification.

• **Weather impacts service.** Ice storms shut down the metroplex completely. Severe thunderstorms cause delays and higher demand for service.

## What Affects the Final Price

• **Vehicle type and size.** Compact cars cost less than full-size trucks. Lifted vehicles, lowered cars, or all-wheel drive may need special equipment, adding $25-50.

• **Distance to destination.** Rates climb quickly beyond 10 miles. Going from downtown Fort Worth to Arlington or Grapevine can push costs over $200.

• **Time and day of service.** Business hours (Monday-Friday, 8 AM-6 PM) offer the best rates. Emergency service costs more, especially during bad weather.

• **Membership discounts.** AAA, insurance roadside coverage, or credit card benefits can cut costs significantly or cover service entirely.

• **Insurance coverage.** Many policies include towing up to $100-150. Check your coverage before calling a private company.

## Stay Safe

• **Exit your vehicle if safely possible.** Get behind a barrier or fence, away from traffic. Fort Worth drivers often don't see stopped vehicles until the last second.

• **Use hazard lights and emergency triangles.** Place reflectors 100 feet behind your vehicle on highways, 50 feet on city streets.

• **Stay visible but protected.** Wear bright colors if you have them. Stand away from your vehicle and oncoming traffic while waiting for the tow truck.

• **Keep doors unlocked for the driver.** Tow truck operators need access to put the vehicle in neutral and steer during loading.
